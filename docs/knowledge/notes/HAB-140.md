### 2026-07-04

**Support workflow decision**

No system — human or AI — can truly verify a past showup happened. The real anti-abuse mechanism is friction and caps, not verification.

Decision: all redemption requests will be reviewed manually by the author for now. When the review load becomes unbearable, consider technical solutions: AI-mediated conversational review or self-attestation with friction (cool-down period + per-pact redemption cap).

**Research questions answered**

| Q | Answer |
|---|---|
| Q1 — Submission UX | Email via About/Feedback screen (HAB-149); no new in-app entity needed |
| Q2 — Support workflow | Manual review by author; escalate to AI-mediated or self-attestation when load grows |
| Q3 — Abuse vectors | Friction + per-pact caps; verification is impossible and unnecessary |
| Q4 — Lightweight version | Self-attestation with cool-down + per-pact redemption cap — viable path when volume grows |
| Q5 — Precedents | No app verifies past activity; all use friction, cost, or reputation as the deterrent |

**Industry precedents**

- **Beeminder** — self-report flag + financial commitment contract as deterrent; no verification
- **Strava** — free manual activity entry; abuse treated as a social/reputational problem
- **Duolingo** — streak freezes as a purchasable entitlement; forgiveness is a product feature, not a support request
- **Habitica** — free past check-off; gamification (character damage) is the only deterrent
- **MyFitnessPal** — free backdating of all logs; pure honour system, zero friction
- **Noom** — human coach can manually adjust user data; closest analogue to our manual-review model
- **Apple Fitness** — zero redemption, no exceptions; strictest stance in the industry
- **Streaks / Habitify** — no redemption; missed = missed

**Pattern:** no app in this space verifies past activity. The lever is always friction, cost, or reputation — never proof. Noom's coach-mediated override is the closest real-world parallel to the HAB-140 approach.

**Debrief note — workflow improvement**

Starting research by surveying existing alternatives first (before forming opinions) proved highly effective here. It grounded the design decisions in real precedents and surfaced the Noom analogy which validated the manual-review approach. Proposal: make "survey existing alternatives first" a standard first step in any research ticket.
