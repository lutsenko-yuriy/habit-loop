## Notes

### 2026-07-20

**Checkup findings may need their own triage workflow**

The 2026-07-20 heavy checkup produced 10 open ledger findings. Before turning them into tickets, we did an ad-hoc pass to (1) find real dependency/impact relationships between findings (not just "same dimension") and draw a directed graph, (2) separate the connected cluster from genuinely independent findings, and (3) decide how to group them into tickets (HAB-187 for the connected cluster, HAB-188 for the independent ones) rather than filing 10 separate tickets or forcing everything into one. None of this triage step exists in `docs/RESEARCH_WORKFLOW.md`, `docs/FEATURE_WORKFLOW.md`, or the `/checkup` skill itself — it was improvised in conversation. Worth raising at debrief: should the checkup process (or a follow-up step after it) formalize a "triage the ledger into tickets" workflow, including the dependency-graph step, instead of leaving it to be reinvented each time a batch of findings is ready to be scheduled?

**Testing rigor for checkup-sourced tickets**

Every finding rolled into HAB-187 (and HAB-188) was originally deferred specifically *because* it touches production logic and needs its own test-writing cycle (per the Boy Scout Rule in `skills/manage/checkup/resources/findings-protocol.md`) — that's the whole reason they weren't fixed inline during the checkup. When implementing these tickets, TDD rigor is not optional/nice-to-have — it's the specific justification for why this work was deferred out of the checkup in the first place. This applies with extra weight to the accessibility items (heavy-8, heavy-9): correctness there should be verified with `flutter_test`'s `meetsGuideline` matchers (tap-target, contrast), not just visual inspection, since those findings exist because the app had *zero* accessibility test coverage to begin with.
