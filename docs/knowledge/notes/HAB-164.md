# HAB-164: Research: on-the-fly localization string updates for post-launch fixes

## Notes

### 2026-07-13

**Survey of alternatives complete**

Ruled out on budget: Lokalise (OTA excluded from Free plan; cheapest paid $120-144/mo), Crowdin (no permanent free tier for private projects; cheapest paid $59/mo — OTA CDN itself is free/generous but useless without a paid project plan), Localazy (200-string free cap vs our current 182 keys leaves no headroom, and CDN/OTA requires a credit card even on "free" tier — recurring-cost risk).

Two credible free candidates remain:
1. **DIY** — extend the already-built `RemoteConfigService`/`getString` (`lib/infrastructure/remote_config/`) to carry a JSON string-override payload; zero new cost, reuses tested no-throw infra, but requires hand-rolling ICU plural resolution (RU has 4 categories) ourselves.
2. **Tolgee self-hosted** — free software (Apache 2.0, 10 seats), native ARB+ICU plural support, true OTA delivery, but requires standing up and maintaining Docker+Postgres infra, which cuts against the solo-dev/no-ops-capacity and pre-launch-simplicity constraints.

POEditor's free tier (1000 strings/5 contributors) fits comfortably but has no OTA SDK — would need the same custom fetch-and-apply client code as the DIY option, just with a translation-editing UI layered on top that's redundant given Yuriy+Claude already edit .arb directly.

## Debrief summary

### 2026-07-13

**What went well**
- The research still saved more effort than it cost: it prevented building and maintaining an OTA localization pipeline (Tolgee self-hosting, or a hand-rolled DIY ICU-plural override) that turned out not to be needed at all.
- The final call — defer, revisit once the app moves to a real store release with a review gate — was the right one.

**What was hard or surprising**
- Worse than expected on process: a full survey across five TMS platforms (Crowdin, Lokalise, Localazy, POEditor, Tolgee) was completed before the load-bearing premise ("need updates within a day") was recognized as already satisfied by the existing multi-times-a-day Firebase App Distribution cadence with no store-review gate. That constraint wasn't listed anywhere before the survey started.
- In hindsight, "OTA / no rebuild needed" was a Could-have, not a Must-have — but the ticket treated it as central from the start, so the survey wasn't prioritized against the goals that actually mattered.

**What to change**
- Add a scoping step to `docs/RESEARCH_WORKFLOW.md` (new step 0, before "survey existing alternatives"): list ticket-specific constraints (including non-functional ones) beyond the general `docs/CONSTRAINTS.md`, and MoSCoW the research goals so survey effort goes to Musts first.
- Promoted the specific constraint that triggered this — no store-review gate, builds ship to testers multiple times a day — into `docs/CONSTRAINTS.md` itself as a standing project fact, since it's reusable across tickets, not just HAB-164-specific.
